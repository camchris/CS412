About Amy Winehouse


Amy Winehouse was a singer-songwriter known for singing in various genres, particularly soul, blues, and jazz. Her debut album "Frank", released in 2003, was a hit in the UK, and her following album, "Back to Black", blew up internationally. Amy Winehouse passed away in 2011 due to alcohol poisoning, at the age of 27.
